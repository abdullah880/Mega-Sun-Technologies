What happens on cloudy days?

Technical Performance

Solar panels still produce electricity on cloudy days, just at reduced capacity. Here's what to expect:

Cloudy Day Performance:

Light clouds:
80% of normal output
Overcast:
25% of normal output
Heavy rain:
10% of normal output

The Grid Connection Advantage

With grid-tied systems (the most common type), you simply draw power from the utility grid when solar production is low. Your home never loses power.

Net Metering Benefits

On sunny days, you often produce excess electricity that gets credited to your account. You use these credits on cloudy days and at night.

Annual Production Reality:

Solar systems are sized based on annual sunlight in your area. Even with cloudy days, a properly sized system will meet your annual energy needs. For example, Seattle (a cloudy city) still has successful solar installations that save homeowners 60-80% on electricity bills.
