In More Detail

Let's break down each step of the solar energy process

The Sun's Energy

The sun produces an enormous amount of energy. In just one hour, enough sunlight reaches the Earth to power the entire world for a year. Solar panels capture a fraction of this energy and convert it into electricity.

Photovoltaic Effect

Solar panels contain photovoltaic cells made of silicon. When sunlight hits these cells, it knocks electrons loose, creating an electric current. This is called the photovoltaic effect.

Power Conversion

Solar panels produce direct current (DC) electricity, but homes and businesses use alternating current (AC). The inverter converts DC to AC, making the electricity compatible with your appliances.

Using the Energy

Once converted to AC, the electricity flows to your electrical panel and powers your lights, appliances, and devices. Any excess energy can be sent back to the grid or stored in batteries.

Key Components of a Solar System

Solar Panels

The energy collectors that capture sunlight and convert it to electricity.

  • Monocrystalline (most efficient)
  • Polycrystalline (cost-effective)
  • 25+ year lifespan

Inverter

Converts DC electricity from panels to AC electricity for your home.

  • String inverters (standard)
  • Microinverters (panel-level)
  • 10-15 year warranty

Mounting Structure

Secures panels to your roof or ground with proper angle and orientation.

  • Roof-mounted (most common)
  • Ground-mounted (flexible)
  • Weather-resistant materials

Optional Batteries

Store excess energy for use at night or during power outages.

  • Lithium-ion (modern)
  • Lead-acid (traditional)
  • Backup power capability

Understanding Net Metering

How you get credits for excess solar energy you produce

Two-Way Energy Flow

Export Extra Units, Get Credits on Your Bill

Net metering is a billing arrangement with your utility company that credits you for the excess electricity your solar panels produce.

During the Day

Your solar panels often produce more electricity than you use. This excess power flows back to the grid.

At Night or Cloudy Days

When your panels aren't producing enough, you draw electricity from the grid using your earned credits.
